# Fabrizio De Palma

Independent Researcher

## Research Themes

- Finite observational structures
- Structural identifiability under bounded observation
- Frontier Field-Dynamics
- Epistemic residue and finite knowledge
- Search and control geometry
- Finite-granularity limits in computation
- Structural topology
- Epistemic and multi-agent interaction

---

## Core Perspective

My work studies finite observational structures, residual non-determinacy,
and local/global control limitations across computer science, complexity theory,
topology, epistemology, systems theory, and adaptive interaction.

The common focus is the analysis of what can be structurally distinguished,
controlled, stabilized, or canonically realized under bounded observation.

### Field–Dynamics: a minimal relational framework
- [Zenodo](https://zenodo.org/records/18473301)

## Computer Science and Complexity

### Finite-Budget Structural Identifiability under Bounded Observation
- [Github](https://github.com/fabrizio-de-palma/Finite-Budget-Structural-Identifiability-under-Bounded-Observation)
- [Zenodo](https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.18736348)

Studies structural identifiability under finite observational budgets, showing
how bounded observation, residual separability, critical identification time,
and maximal identifiable scale constrain regime recognition.

### Probe Complexity of Local Model Statistics in Sharp-SAT
- [Zenodo](https://zenodo.org/records/19288624)

Investigates how much information about local model statistics in Sharp-SAT can
be extracted through finite counting probes, distinguishing abstract probing
power from realizable probing constraints.


### P vs NP: Finite-Grain Blindness, Completion, and Canonical Realization
- [Zenodo](https://zenodo.org/records/20031554)

Develops a finite-grain perspective on P vs NP, focusing on observational
blindness, completion-level structure, and the possible failure of canonical
realization at the boundary of computational distinguishability.

## Frontier Concept

### Frontier Field-Dynamics: Structural Limits of Reasoning under Finite Observational Granularity
- [Zenodo](https://zenodo.org/records/18498021)

Introduces Frontier Field-Dynamics as a framework for analyzing reasoning
processes under fixed observational granularity, identifying frontiers where
internal evolution no longer yields observable informational progress.

### Frontier Field-Dynamics in Action: Structural Impossibility Results under Fixed Observational Granularity
- [Zenodo](https://zenodo.org/records/18509375)

Applies Frontier Field-Dynamics to concrete mathematical and computational
settings, deriving structural impossibility results related to finite-grain
stabilization, non-factorization, and failure of canonical selection.

## Epistemology and Strategic Interaction

### Epistemic Residue: Knowledge, Certainty, and the Structuring of What Remains
- [Zenodo](https://zenodo.org/records/19098227)

Develops the concept of epistemic residue as the structured remainder generated
by finite knowledge, arguing that knowledge does not simply eliminate the
unknown but organizes internal zones of non-determination.

### Identity as the Dynamic Center of Human Motivation
- [Zenodo](https://zenodo.org/records/19476473)

Proposes a structural account of identity as a dynamic motivational center,
linking subjective coherence, transformation, aspiration, and internal
self-organization.

### Epistemic Map Games (EMG): Strategic Interaction without a Shared Objective Game
- [Zenodo](https://zenodo.org/records/18475668)

Introduces Epistemic Map Games as a framework for strategic interaction in which
agents act through subjective epistemic maps rather than a single shared
objective game.
